1Concept Briefing

This continues the story of Sabita Handloom & Trading Co. from the Journal Entries & Ledger lab. Read this once carefully — you'll apply every idea here in the next four stages.

What a Trial Balance Actually Is

A trial balance is simply every ledger account balance, listed in one place — Debit balances in one column, Credit balances in the other. It is not a financial statement; it is a check. If total Debits equal total Credits, the arithmetic of double entry has held up so far (it does not prove there are no errors — only that debits and credits match). If they don't match, something has gone wrong somewhere in the ledger, and that mismatch is your first clue for tracing it.

From Trial Balance to Financial Statements

StatementWhat goes into itWhat it tells you
Trading AccountDirect trading items: Sales, Purchases, Opening/Closing StockGross Profit — profit from core buying & selling, before overheads
Profit & Loss AccountIndirect expenses & incomes: Rent, Salary, Commission Received, etc.Net Profit — Gross Profit minus running costs
Balance SheetAssets, Liabilities, Capital, Drawings, Net Profit carried inWhat the business owns and owes on a given date

(Trilingual note: "Trial Balance," "Trading Account," and "Gross/Net Profit" are flagged to remain in English with a one-line gloss for the Odia/Hindi voiceover — no natural single-word equivalent in common commercial usage.)

4Statement Builder

Using the fully corrected Quarter 2 trial balance — whichever single account you corrected in Stage 3, with everything else exactly as drafted — and a Closing Stock of ₹52,000, build the Trading Account, Profit & Loss Account, and Balance Sheet. Enter each figure and check your work.

Before you enter a single figure: go back and re-confirm the corrected trial balance actually tallies — Total Debits should equal Total Credits. If you're not sure which account you corrected or what its true value was, click here to return to Stage 3 and check, rather than guessing. The same habit applies once you've built the statements below: a real accountant never submits a Balance Sheet without first checking that Total Assets actually equals Total Liabilities + Capital — if it doesn't, the fix is to go back and find the error, never to force a match by adjusting a random figure.

Trading Account

Sales ₹2,38,000 + Closing Stock ₹52,000 (Credit side) − Purchases ₹1,62,000 (Debit side) = Gross Profit c/d

Profit & Loss Account

Gross Profit b/d − Rent ₹8,500 − Salary ₹21,000 = Net Profit
