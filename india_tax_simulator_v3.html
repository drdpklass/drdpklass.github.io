ℹ️ Important Notes for FY 2025–26

New Tax Regime (Default)

The New Tax Regime is now the default regime for all taxpayers from FY 2023-24 onwards. Rebate u/s 87A increased — no tax up to ₹12 lakh income (₹12.75L for salaried with standard deduction).

Standard Deduction

₹75,000 Standard Deduction available under New Regime (increased from ₹50,000). Old Regime retains ₹50,000.

Key Budget 2025 Changes

• TDS threshold on interest raised to ₹1 lakh (senior citizens)
• 87A rebate: Up to ₹12L taxable income = zero tax
• MSME credit guarantees enhanced
• NPS Vatsalya for minors introduced

🖥️ IMS — Invoice Management System (GST 2.0 Oct 2024)

How IMS Works

1. Supplier files GSTR-1 / 1A → Invoices appear in your IMS dashboard
2. You can: Accept ✓ | Reject ✗ | Pending ⏳
3. Only Accepted invoices flow to GSTR-2B and give ITC
4. Pending invoices: ITC conditionally available but risky
5. Rejected: No ITC. Supplier must issue credit note

⚠️ IMS Critical Warning

If supplier does NOT file GSTR-1, invoices won't appear in your IMS. ITC cannot be claimed. Always verify supplier compliance before payment to avoid ITC loss.

🔀 QRMP Scheme — Quarterly Return Monthly Payment

Who can opt QRMP?

Taxpayers with aggregate turnover up to ₹5 crore in preceding financial year. File GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B quarterly. But pay tax MONTHLY using PMT-06 challan.

ActivityFrequencyDue Date
GSTR-1 (IFF — optional)Monthly13th of next month (M1, M2)
GSTR-1 (Full)Quarterly13th of month after quarter
PMT-06 (Tax Payment)Monthly (M1, M2)25th of next month
GSTR-3BQuarterly22nd / 24th of month after Q

Monthly Payment Methods (QRMP)

Fixed Sum Method: Pay 35% of last quarter's tax liability
Self-Assessment Method: Calculate actual monthly liability and pay
Choose whichever is applicable — portal auto-fills PMT-06.

QRMP Advantage

• Quarterly return filing (less compliance burden)
• Optional IFF (Invoice Furnishing Facility) to push B2B invoices to buyer's GSTR-2B monthly
• Small businesses benefit from reduced filing frequency

💡 GST Planning — Legal Tax Savings Strategies
🎯
Opt for Composition Scheme if eligible

If turnover is ≤ ₹1.5 Cr and mostly B2C (no ITC-intensive purchases), Composition @1% (traders) saves significant GST vs regular 18%. No monthly compliance burden.

Potential saving: Up to 17% of turnover on output tax
📋
Register under LUT for exports — save IGST

Export under Letter of Undertaking (LUT) means you don't pay IGST upfront. Avoids blocking working capital. Claim ITC refund instead. Renew LUT every April.

Saves 0% IGST outflow on all export supplies
🔄
Maximise ITC on all business purchases

Ensure every eligible purchase has a valid GST invoice with correct GSTIN. Verify supplier GSTR-2B. Missing invoices = lost ITC permanently. Reconcile monthly.

Every ₹1 of ITC = ₹1 reduction in GST payable
🏭
Claim ITC on capital goods in one shot

Unlike income tax (depreciation spread), ITC on capital goods (plant, machinery) can be claimed in full in the month of purchase. No deferral — claim immediately.

Immediate cash flow benefit on large capital expenditure
📦
Split supply classification — avoid highest slab

If a supply has both goods and services, correctly classify as composite/mixed supply. Principal supply determines the rate. Avoid treating as highest-rate supply by mistake.

Can reduce rate from 18% to 12% or 5% if correctly classified
🔁
Use RCM strategically for GTA services

If using GTA services, ensure RCM is paid and ITC is claimed (if eligible). GTA can opt to pay GST themselves (forward charge @12% with ITC), which may be better in some cases.

Proper RCM handling avoids double taxation
🏢
Job work — send goods without GST liability

Goods sent to job worker are NOT a supply — no GST applicable. Principal must receive goods back within 1 year (inputs) or 3 years (capital goods). Perfect for manufacturing.

Zero GST on goods sent for job work — major cash flow benefit
🏬
Claim ITC on works contract for plant & machinery

Works contract services on plant and machinery are NOT blocked under Sec 17(5). Only immovable property construction is blocked. Correctly classify to recover ITC.

Recover 18% GST on factory/plant construction (machinery portion)
📑
File GSTR-9 even if not mandatory (small taxpayers)

Voluntary GSTR-9 filing helps reconcile any ITC mismatches, correct errors in monthly returns, and avoid future scrutiny. No penalty for voluntary filing.

Avoids 3-year look-back risk and demand notices
⏰
Claim ITC within time limit — don't lose it

ITC must be claimed by 30 November of next FY or date of filing annual return (whichever is earlier). Missed ITC = permanent loss. Reconcile invoices every month.

Never lose ITC by monitoring GSTR-2B every month

u/s 80CCD(2): Employer NPS up to 14% of basic is deductible even under New Regime. Negotiate this into your CTC — it costs employer nothing extra (they get deduction too).

💳
PMVVY for senior citizens — guaranteed 7.4% for 10 years

Pradhan Mantri Vaya Vandana Yojana (LIC): Max ₹15L investment, guaranteed 7.4% pension for 10 years. Senior citizens should consider alongside SCSS 8.2%.

Guaranteed pension stream — low risk for retired investors
🌐
Diversify with International Mutual Funds

Allocate 10–15% of equity portfolio to international funds (Nasdaq, S&P 500). Taxed at slab rate now (post Apr 2023 debt MF rule) but offers USD appreciation + global diversification.

Rupee depreciation alone adds 3–4% to returns historically

📚 Indian Tax Glossary & Key Sections

Quick reference to Income Tax Act 1961 & GST Act 2017 terms

📖 Income Tax — Key Sections
Section 10 — Exemptions

10(10D): Life insurance proceeds exempt
10(13A): HRA exemption for salaried
10(14): Special allowances (LTA, children education)
10(17A): Awards by Government
10(38): LTCG on equity (now u/s 112A)

Section 80C — Investment Deductions

Max deduction: ₹1,50,000
Includes: EPF, PPF, ELSS, NSC, 5-yr FD, SSY, LIC premium, home loan principal, tuition fees, ULIP, Senior Citizen Savings Scheme, NPS (80CCD(1))

Section 80D — Medical Insurance

Self & Family: ₹25,000 (₹50,000 if senior citizen)
Parents: ₹25,000 (₹50,000 if senior citizen)
Preventive check-up: ₹5,000 (within above limits)
Max possible: ₹1,00,000 (both senior citizens)

Section 87A — Tax Rebate

New Regime (FY 2025-26): Rebate up to ₹60,000 if taxable income ≤ ₹12,00,000. Effectively zero tax up to ₹12L.
Old Regime: Rebate of ₹12,500 if taxable income ≤ ₹5,00,000.

Section 112A — LTCG on Equity

LTCG on listed equity shares/equity MF units held > 12 months:
• Exempt up to ₹1,25,000 (FY 2024-25 onwards)
• Taxed at 12.5% on gains exceeding ₹1.25L
• No indexation benefit

Section 24 — Home Loan Interest

Self-occupied property: Max ₹2,00,000 deduction
Let-out property: Full interest deductible
Available only in Old Regime. Pre-construction interest in 5 equal installments.

Section 44AD / 44ADA — Presumptive Tax

44AD: Business with turnover ≤ ₹2 Cr — declare 8% (6% for digital receipts) as profit
44ADA: Professionals (doctor, CA, lawyer) receipts ≤ ₹75L — declare 50% as income
No audit required if opted

🧾 GST — Key Terms
Types of GST

CGST: Central GST (collected by Centre, intra-state)
SGST: State GST (collected by State, intra-state)
IGST: Integrated GST (inter-state, imports, collected by Centre, shared with states)
UTGST: For Union Territories

GST Registration Threshold

Normal States: ₹40 lakh (goods), ₹20 lakh (services)
Special Category States: ₹20 lakh (goods), ₹10 lakh (services)
E-commerce operators: Compulsory registration regardless of turnover

Composition Scheme

Available for turnover ≤ ₹1.5 Cr:
• Manufacturers/Traders: 1% (0.5% CGST + 0.5% SGST)
• Restaurant (non-alcohol): 5% (2.5%+2.5%)
• Service providers: 6% (3%+3%)
Cannot claim ITC. Quarterly return (CMP-08).

Input Tax Credit (ITC)

GST paid on inputs can offset output liability.
Blocked u/s 17(5): Motor vehicles (personal), food/beverages, clubs, health services, works contract for immovable property, personal use items.
Must file GSTR-2B reconciliation monthly.

GST Returns

GSTR-1: Outward supplies (11th of next month)
GSTR-3B: Monthly summary return (20th of next month)
GSTR-9: Annual return (31 December)
GSTR-9C: Reconciliation statement (turnover > ₹5 Cr)

Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM)

In RCM, the recipient (not supplier) pays GST. Applies to:
• Goods Transport Agency services
• Legal services by advocate
• Import of services
• Specified goods notified by Government
ITC available on RCM paid for business use.

E-Way Bill

Required for movement of goods > ₹50,000 in value.
• Generated on GST portal before movement
• Valid for 1 day per 100 km (for normal cargo)
• Not required for exempt goods, non-motorised vehicles, intra-city movement
